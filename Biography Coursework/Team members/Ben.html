4: Engineer, Nakidel Yamine Ben Keldi

Background

Nakidel Ben whose full name is Nakidel Yamine Ben Keldi is currently working under UNICEF, an organization dedicated to improving the lives of children and their families worldwide. He hails from Madagascar, a country known for its rich biodiversity and unique cultural heritage. His work, primarily, focuses on child welfare, education, and health initiatives aimed at fostering sustainable development in his home country.

He pursued his higher education at the University of Antananarivo in Madagascar, where he earned a Bachelor’s degree in Social Sciences with a concentration in Development Studies. This academic foundation provided him with critical insights into social issues affecting children and communities in Madagascar. Following his undergraduate studies, he furthered his education by obtaining a Master’s degree in International Development from a reputable university abroad, which equipped him with advanced knowledge and skills necessary for effective program implementation within international organizations like UNICEF.


Achievements

His contributions mainly reflect to UNICEF and the broader community include developing programs that address malnutrition among children under five years old, which has significantly reduced child mortality rates in targeted regions.

Furthermore, he has collaborated with local governments to implement training workshops for educators on inclusive teaching practices, ensuring that all children have access to quality education regardless of their background or abilities.
