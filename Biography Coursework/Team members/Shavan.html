8: Engineer, Ssesaazi Shavan

Background

Ssesaazi Ismael was born on February 23, 2001, in Gayaza, Kyankwanzi district, Uganda. A Ugandan national, he is one of the eleven children of Mr. Kityo Swaib and Mrs. Nakimuli Mastula, both residents of Bugologolo-Gayaza in Kyankwanzi. As the middle child, Ssesaazi grew up within a family renowned for its noble ties and leadership in the community.
His grandfather, known by his nickname "Kamasu" and formally named Eriasa Kamuli, played a prominent role in the local society. A close friend of the late King Muteesa II of Buganda, Kamuli and the King often spent leisure time hunting together. This bond led to Kamuli being appointed Chief of Gayaza Bugologolo in Kyankwanzi in 1962


Achievements

  • Despite having both parents alive, Ssesaazi’s early years were marked by considerable challenges. In 2006, he faced a serious health crisis due to a severe colonic illness. That same year, his mother moved with him from their rural village to Kasubi, an urban center in Kampala District, in search of better opportunities, but this transition brought additional difficulties.
  • Ssesaazi was recognized as a diligent and resilient student, although financial constraints meant he attended multiple primary and secondary schools to minimize the costs. He completed his Primary Leaving Examination (PLE) at St. Charles Lwanga Primary School in Kyankwanzi District, excelling academically and ranking as the third-best student in his division
  • For secondary school, Ssesaazi attended Light College Secondary School in Kiboga District, later transferring to Kampala High School in Kampala District, where he sat for both the Uganda Certificate of Education (UCE) and the Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education (UACE). His time at Kampala High School marked significant educational achievements.

Key Contributions

In 2021, Ssesaazi enrolled at Victoria University, Uganda, during a time of national transition due to the presidential elections. Opting to pursue a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, he is focused on building a career in technology, aiming to use his knowledge to make meaningful contributions. Currently, he is on track to graduate with the Class of 2025, embodying a commitment to realizing his dreams in the field of computer science.
